Yo-Yo:

History

The exact origin of yo-yos is uncertain as it is still under debate to this day, but they most likely originated from China. However, the first notable historical mention of yo-yos came from Greece in the year 500 BCE. In 1860 the word yo-yo was recognized in a Filipino dictionary. In 1923 a Filipino man named Pedro Flores began making and selling yo-yos in America. Because the youth loved his toy he established a yo-yo factory in California in 1928. Here, he trademarked the name of the toy as a “Yo-Yo”. In order to sell the toys he hired yo-yo trick “masters” and hosted many competitions. This was how Donald F. Duncan sought the yo-yo’s potential and created and designed the yo-yos as we know them today.
